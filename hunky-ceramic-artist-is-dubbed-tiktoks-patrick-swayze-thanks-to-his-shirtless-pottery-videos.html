Hunky ceramic artist is dubbed TikTok's Patrick Swayze thanks to his shirtless pottery videos

February 2024 · 6 minute read

A handsome TikTok star is earning comparisons to Patrick Swayze’s iconic character in Ghost after his pottery videos turned him into a viral sensation.

Ceramic artist Theo Hirschfield, 20, from San Diego, California, started sharing footage of himself sculpting clay vases, pots, and bowls a little over a year ago, and fans have since dubbed him the real-life Sam Wheat.   

Now his inbox is inundated with flirtatious messages from men and women longing to re-enact the sexy pottery scene from the 1990 supernatural film starring Swayze and Demi Moore.

Made for the movies: The clips have earned Hirschfield (left) comparisons to Patrick Swayze’s character in Ghost, Sam Wheat (right)

Passion: The potter was first introduced to ceramics during his senior year of high school and ‘fell in love with the concept of manipulating a piece of the earth into art’

‘I was first introduced into ceramics during my senior year of high school where I took an introductory ceramics class and soon fell in love with the concept of manipulating a piece of the earth into art,’ Hirschfield said.

‘I loved how I had complete control over how I wanted a certain piece to look and as I would consider myself a very physically orientated person. The fact that ceramics is so hands-on explains why I’m obsessed with it.’

In March 2018, he decided to purchase a throwing wheel so that he could start perfecting his pottery. He made vases, bowls, and cups as well as other crockery pieces. 

As he got better over time, Hirschfield invested in a kiln, glazes, shelving and eventually had a fully functioning pottery studio in his parent’s back garden.  

‘Although my parents helped fund my new hobby, I also saved up some money working at a fish market and in total, I spent around $18,000 over the course of a year investing in my hobby,’ he said.

Hirschfield started taking his passion for pottery more seriously after his parents, Marc, 54, and Anise, 55, posted some photos of his artwork on Facebook and people asked to purchase it.

‘I sold my first white bowl for $30, which felt incredible,’ he said. ‘I couldn’t believe someone wanted to purchase my pottery.

‘This really kickstarted me to make more art and I began filming myself making pottery and posting these videos online.’

The first video he posted showed him casually throwing a vase, which gained around 300 views on Instagram at the time. 

Although he was overwhelmed by the attention, his friends Brooke Odmark, 21, and Matthew Moses, 21, suggested he share the clips on TikTok as well. That’s when his career started to take off. 

Hirschfield, who is known as @thclay on TikTok, earned over four million views and more than 90,000 followers in his first three months on the platform. 

Shortly after, his videos started going viral with one reaching over 20 million views. Last week, he celebrated reaching one million followers.

‘I couldn’t believe the amount of attention I was getting from across the world and I still feel super lucky to be able to have such a supportive following,’ Hirschfield said.

However, the potter admitted he sometimes feels ‘quite overwhelmed by the pressure to entertain, which led him to take a two-month hiatus from social media in November 2020. 

‘I was going through a really hard break up,’ he explained. ‘I had lots of previous anxiety as well as depression that I had never faced and taking a break from TikTok really gave me the space to overcome these battles.

‘At the end of the day, I make this content for my fans and to help me promote myself as well as my brand, which is what matters the most.’

Hirschfield said people will leave comments on his videos, saying things like, ‘You forgot a shirt’ or ‘This is giving me Ghost vibes,’ while comparing him to Swayze. 

Sometimes the comments are more suggestive. He recalled one person telling him: ‘I’ve never wanted to be a lump of clay so bad.’

Uncomfortable: Hirschfield said a lot of the comments on his videos are ‘funny,’  but some of the outrageously suggestive ones make him ‘uncomfortable’ 

Goals:  ‘As I’m still in college, I’m trying to balance my time between TikTok and my studies, but I hope to get my work into more galleries in the future,’ he said

‘I definitely feed into this type of attention with the shirtless pottery videos I make,’ Hirschfield admitted. ‘However, some people do take it too far, and – especially at first – some of the comments and messages I was getting made me feel uncomfortable.

‘I try not to pay too much attention to it, however,  and I mostly focus on the light-hearted comments, as well as making my content.’

Hirschfield, who born with profound hearing loss, said there have also been people on TikTok who have supported him throughout his journey. 

‘As I used to struggle with being accepted as normal, the response received from hundreds of people reaching out, saying that they’ve been inspired by my story, was incredibly empowering,’ he said. 

‘I’m glad that I’ve been given this opportunity to share my story and hopefully inspire others who may have also felt held back in life due to their hearing loss, and I have my fanbase on TikTok to thank largely for this.

‘As I’m still in college, I’m trying to balance my time between TikTok and my studies, but I hope to get my work into more galleries in the future and keep entertaining people as I enjoy making others happy.’
